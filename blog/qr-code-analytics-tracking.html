Tracking QR Code Performance: Metrics That Matter

May 12, 2026 9 min read

If you're not tracking your QR code scans, you're missing out on a wealth of data that could help you optimize your marketing efforts. Understanding how users interact with your codes is essential for continuous improvement.

Key Metrics to Follow

  • Scan Count: The total number of times your QR code has been scanned. This is the most basic measure of engagement.
  • Unique Scans: The number of individual users who have scanned your code. This helps you understand your actual reach.
  • Location Data: Where your scans are coming from. This can help you identify which physical locations are performing best.
  • Time of Scan: When users are scanning your codes. Use this to determine the best times to run promotions or update content.
  • Device Type: Whether users are on iOS or Android. This information can help you optimize the landing page experience.

How to Track Your Codes

While static QR codes don't offer built-in tracking, you can use URL shorteners or UTM parameters to track the traffic they generate to your website. For more advanced tracking, dynamic QR codes are the way to go, as they allow you to change the destination URL and track scans in real-time.

Turning Data into Action

Use the data you collect to make informed decisions. If a specific location isn't generating scans, consider moving the QR code to a more visible spot. If most of your users are scanning on weekends, plan your biggest promotions accordingly.

Data-driven marketing is the key to success in 2026. By tracking the metrics that matter, you can ensure your QR code campaigns are as effective as possible.

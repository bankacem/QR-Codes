QR Code Security: How to Protect Your Users

Feb 2, 2026 6 min read

As QR codes become more prevalent, so do the security risks associated with them. "Quishing" (QR code phishing) is a real threat that both businesses and consumers need to be aware of. Protecting your users starts with understanding the risks and implementing best practices.

Common QR Code Security Risks

The primary risk is that a user can't visually verify the destination of a QR code before scanning it. Attackers can replace legitimate QR codes in public places with malicious ones that lead to phishing sites or trigger malware downloads.

Best Practices for Businesses

  • Use Branded Links: When possible, use QR codes that lead to your own domain and display the destination URL clearly.
  • Physical Inspections: Regularly check your physical QR codes (on posters, tables, or storefronts) to ensure they haven't been tampered with or covered by stickers.
  • Educate Your Users: Provide tips on your website about what a legitimate interaction with your brand looks like.
  • Secure Generation: Use trusted QR code generators like QRGen Pro that do not store your data or inject unwanted redirects.

Advice for Consumers

Always be cautious when scanning QR codes in public places. Use a scanner that shows the URL preview before opening it. If a QR code is asking for sensitive information like passwords or credit card details immediately after scanning, it is likely a scam.

Security is a shared responsibility. By staying informed and vigilant, we can continue to enjoy the convenience of QR codes safely.
